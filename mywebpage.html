Thomas J.Watson


Thomas J. Watson, Sr. is the American industialist, who built the International Business Machines Corporation (IBM) into the largest manufacturer of electric typewriters and data-processing equipment in the world.

  • In 1939, he received an honorary degree in Doctor of Commercial Science from the Oglethorpe University.
  • In the 1940s, Watson was on the national executive board of the Boy Scouts of America.
  • Watson served as a trustee of Columbia Universitry from June 6, 1933, until his death.
  • He was posthumously inducted into the Junior Achievement U.S. Business Hall of Fame in 1990.
